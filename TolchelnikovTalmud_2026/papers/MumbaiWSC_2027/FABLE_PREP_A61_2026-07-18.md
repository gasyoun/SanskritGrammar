## Revised argument architecture

**Question:** Why did CDSL remain corrigible after its founding department
disappeared and its principal developer retired?

**Thesis:** CDSL endured by repeatedly converting fragile personal or
institutional scholarly labour into recoverable shared infrastructure.

**Causal sequence:**

1. institutional funding converted books into data;
2. collaboration converted character-coded transcription into shared structure;
3. archival custody detached survival from the founding department;
4. public versioning externalised decisions and corrections;
5. stable files and identifiers made succession technically possible;
6. maintenance remained socially concentrated, so transferability is necessary
   but not sufficient for sustainability.

**Rival explanations to keep visible:** institutional custody explains hosting
but not correction; openness explains access but not maintainability; heroic
individuals explain work done but not why successors can recover it.
