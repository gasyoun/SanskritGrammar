# 9. Limits of the Evidence

_Created: 18-07-2026 · Last updated: 18-07-2026_

This report combines three evidence classes that answer different questions.
Committed data and code support exact claims about the surviving repository and
correction records. Published project reports and attributed archival or oral
testimony support claims about decisions, motives, and institutional
transitions. The causal argument is an interpretation of those two classes; it
is not itself a computed result.

Five limits follow.

1. **The public record begins late.** CDSL began in 1994, while the GitHub
   organisation and the reconstructable correction series begin in 2014. The
   record is therefore strongest for the last third of the project's history.
2. **Survival is selective.** Repository history records committed work. It
   misses failed experiments, private negotiation, tacit maintenance, and
   corrections that were never logged. The absence of a commit is not evidence
   that no work occurred.
3. **Identities are not interchangeable.** OBS-T corrector labels, Git logins,
   correction implementers, and historically named participants are different
   populations. The report does not estimate a single total number of people
   who contributed to CDSL.
4. **The snapshot has multiple cutoffs.** The article release is
   `a61-history-v1.1` (calendar alias `2026-07`), but the GitHub snapshot is
   dated 1 June 2026 and OBS-T ends on 30 May 2026. Cross-repository
   measurements have per-row July verification dates. Later activity is outside
   the quantitative envelope.
5. **Preservation is not maintenance.** Versioned, licensed files make recovery
   possible; they do not guarantee that a server remains secure, corrections
   are adjudicated, or contributors are recruited. The paper's central claim is
   about increased recoverability, not solved sustainability.

The structured milestone layer also contains two `evidence_pending` slots for
historical email. Until the archive is supplied and reviewed, those rows cannot
support load-bearing claims. The 2013 transfer to the DCH now has public
institutional locators; the private-email milestones remain outside the
load-bearing argument.
