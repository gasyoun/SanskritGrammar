## Kapp–Malten 1997 PDF verification — gate (b) — 19-07-2026 ([H1317](https://github.com/gasyoun/Uprava/blob/main/handoffs/H1317-Fable_SanskritGrammar_a61-kapp-malten-pdf-verification_19.07.26.md), Fable 5 `claude-fable-5`)

Every Kapp–Malten quotation was checked word-by-word against the project PDF (https://www.sanskrit-lexicon.uni-koeln.de/CDSL.pdf, 10 pp.). Results:

1. **Block quote in §1 — page locator CONFIRMED (p. 1), fair-dealing PASS, but the quote was NOT verbatim as claimed — now fixed (vetoable, VF1/VF2).** Two silent deviations from the PDF's abstract were restored to the original: "to provide easy access" → "to provide **an** easy access" (VF1) and "All these **have been** structured **and mostly** unified" → "All these **will be** structured and unified" (VF2 — the paraphrase had changed the 1997 authors' future-tense plan into an accomplished fact, a real meaning shift in a quote presented as verbatim). The original's "Monier-William's" apostrophe stands as printed. Fair dealing: ~150 words of a publicly self-hosted 10-page conference report, attributed, quoted for review — no concern.
2. **§7.2 misquote — fixed (vetoable, VF3).** The paper quoted "MW has largely followed the order of meanings in **PWG**"; the PDF (p. 6, footnote) reads "It may be surmised that MW has largely followed the order of meanings in **PW1**" — a hedged footnote, and the siglum inside the quotation marks had been silently rewritten. Now quoted verbatim with the hedge, the p. 6 locator, and a parenthetical siglum gloss (PW1 = PWG).
3. **§3.3 quotation — REPORT ONLY, file frozen byte-identical by the author's ruling.** The §3.3 quote "it seems desirable to have high-quality Sanskrit databases freely available to all researchers" normalises the PDF's own typography: p. 9 reads "it seems desirable to **to** have **high quality** Sanskrit databases freely **availabe** to all researchers" (doubled "to", unhyphenated "high quality", misspelt "availabe"). The cleaned quotation is defensible, but strict practice wants either verbatim + [sic] or a "typography normalised" note, plus the missing p. 9 locator. §3.3 stays untouched under the byte-identical ruling — a human decides whether to amend it at submission formatting.
4. **§3.1 source tension — REPORT ONLY, substance.** §3.1 states the original digital MW "was typed in Azhivaikkal … by professional typists working from page images". The 1997 report itself (p. 2) says MW was **OCR-scanned** on a Kurzweil K5000 in Cologne (~70% accuracy, J. Tümmers, mid-1995) and the files then "sent to India for proofreading and correction". Double-entry typing is documented in the report's account only for later material, and §3.1's own Burnouf example. Either §3.1 conflates MW's OCR origin with the typing regime of the later dictionaries, or it rests on later testimony that supersedes the 1997 account — the author holds the sources to adjudicate. Until resolved, the sentence contradicts the primary source the same paragraph cites.

Gate (b) is otherwise CLOSED: p. 1 locator verified, fair-dealing passed. Remaining to 5/5: the §3.3/§3.1 rulings above (author), §8.1 roles read-and-confirm, submission-time abstract wording.
