## Major findings (block submission — both applied this pass)

### M1 — Under-cited field; the novelty claim overreaches. §2 Related work.
**Where.** §2 "The gap": *"An ACL Anthology crosswalk finds no paper that adjudicates a grammar's
categorical rule against corpus frequency… We state these as honest non-findings."*
**Why a referee objects.** The non-finding is scoped to the ACL Anthology, but the paper's move —
measuring a pedagogical text against corpus frequency — is an established subfield *outside* ACL:
corpus-based evaluation of teaching materials (vocabulary/grammar coverage vs corpus frequency;
[systematic review](https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/EJ1472529.pdf),
[L2-textbook lexis](https://www.academypublication.com/issues2/jltr/vol09/03/03.pdf),
[frequency-as-yardstick](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0346251X1500038X)),
and the descriptive-vs-prescriptive / *rules-as-regularities* corpus-grammar tradition (Biber-style
corpus grammar). A corpus-linguistics or lexicography referee rejects on field-unawareness. The
"no precedent" framing invites it.
**Fix (applied).** Rewrote §2 to add the two traditions and re-scope the novelty precisely: the
contribution is not "compare a textbook to a corpus" but (a) the target = a reference grammar's
*categorical rules* (not vocabulary lists), (b) the two-axis fact/pedagogy design + the divergence
typology, (c) the Sanskrit *seṭ/aniṭ* target, for which the ACL non-finding genuinely holds.
**Status: APPLIED.**

### M2 — Central finding rests on a single annotator; no inter-annotator agreement. Limitations / §4.5.
**Where.** §4.5 and §6. The headline result (divergence class ↔ pedagogy verdict) is a correspondence
between two *subjective* codings — `verdict_fact`/`verdict_pedagogy` (H768) and the divergence class
(H773) — both produced by the same author/pipeline. The existing anti-circularity caveat addresses
*shared signal* but not the absence of an *independent coder*.
**Why a referee objects.** For a judgment-based coding task, a single annotator with no reported
agreement is a standard validity threat and an ARR-checklist reproducibility gap; the paper's core
claim is exactly the kind that a second coder is expected to corroborate.
**Fix (applied).** Named it explicitly in Limitations as a validity threat, and pointed to a
second-coder study on a sample (and the cross-grammar re-annotation) as the remedy. Kept the finding
descriptive, not causal.
**Status: APPLIED.**
