### Added
- **Scherzl's 1 168 case-government relations adjudicated against the DCS treebank (H1372, Opus 4.8 `claude-opus-4-8`).** Every governed-case relation in [`government_lexicon.jsonl`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritGrammar/blob/main/BuhlerLeitfaden_1923/government_class_index/government_lexicon.jsonl) is checked, root by root, against the [dcs-conllu](https://github.com/gasyoun/dcs-conllu) dependency treebank (15 900 CoNLL-U files, 5.69 M tokens, 754 726 sentences) — new [`GOVERNMENT_VS_DCS_ADJUDICATION.md`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritGrammar/blob/main/BuhlerLeitfaden_1923/government_class_index/GOVERNMENT_VS_DCS_ADJUDICATION.md), [`government_vs_dcs_adjudication.jsonl`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritGrammar/blob/main/BuhlerLeitfaden_1923/government_class_index/government_vs_dcs_adjudication.jsonl) (per-relation verdict + evidence), [`dcs_verb_government_profiles.json`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritGrammar/blob/main/BuhlerLeitfaden_1923/government_class_index/dcs_verb_government_profiles.json) (corpus valency frame per verb), and the [`adjudicate_government_vs_dcs.py`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritGrammar/blob/main/BuhlerLeitfaden_1923/government_class_index/adjudicate_government_vs_dcs.py) + [`aggregate_dcs_gov.py`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritGrammar/blob/main/BuhlerLeitfaden_1923/government_class_index/aggregate_dcs_gov.py) builders.
  - **Result: Scherzl's 19th-century government catalogue is strongly corroborated.** Two evidence tiers — a direct dependency **government arc** (case-marked nominal as a child of the verb; strong) and **co-occurrence** (weak, informative only when zero). Verdicts: **693 CONFIRMED (59.3 %)**, 280 COOCCURRENCE (24.0 %), **9 UNATTESTED (0.8 %)**, 186 ABSENT (15.9 %). On the **982 adjudicable** relations (root present as a bare DCS verb), **70.6 % are directly confirmed** and **99.1 % find at least co-occurrence**, with **zero high-frequency contradictions** — no well-attested verb whose Scherzl-claimed case never appears with it.
  - **Honest caveats baked into the report.** Only **~3.9 % of DCS sentences carry dependency arcs**, so a zero dep-count is not disconfirming — that is why the confirmation rate is a lower bound. Each relation matches its **most specific** DCS lemma (reading-stem first, with anusvāra `ṁ→ṃ` normalisation recovering 11 prefixed verbs like `saṃdhā`/`praśaṃs`/`śaṃs`; bare root only as fallback) — never summing stem + root, which would credit a prefixed reading with the bare root's frame. The ABSENT bucket is dominated by lemma-form mismatches (Scherzl guṇa `sarj` vs DCS `sṛj`), not missing verbs; closing it needs a stem→root normaliser (guṇa/vṛddhi undo), left as a follow-up.
