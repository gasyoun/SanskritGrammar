## Methodology synthesis — are the author's presentation principles justified?

On the 64-claim verified core (of a 404-candidate full-book harvest; five entries additionally double-verified by the concurrent seed pass BU-1..BU-5), Bühler comes out of the two-axis check remarkably clean: 58 TRUE · 4 OVERSTATED · 1 FALSE · 1 UNTESTABLE. His signature virtue is *calibrated hedging*. Where Kochergina's failure mode was the absolute quantifier ("всегда", "только", "по единому правилу") over a known exception class, Bühler's hedges repeatedly land exactly on the variable zone and his absolutes on the categorical one: "обыкновенно" for the optional visarga assimilations (HB-4), "почти все" for a ten-member class with one exception handled in the same lesson (HB-50), "иногда" for a form the corpus puts at 0.95% (HB-22), "очень редкая" for one at 0.4% (HB-42), closed inventories given complete (HB-18, HB-36, HB-52) and non-exhaustive lists not claimed exhaustive (HB-11). The clearest head-to-head: the same corpus number — the seṭ future -iṣya at 56.8% of distinct forms — that graded Kochergina's "по единому правилу -syá" OVERSTATED (HK-4) grades Bühler's "либо непосредственно, либо при посредстве i" TRUE (HB-59). The fact is the same; the calibration differs.

Three failure modes survive, all instructive. **(1) The Урок-I absolute.** The beginner-facing opening states that all ten tense/mood categories have both voices (HB-2) — contradicted by the book's own later lessons (root and siṣ-aorists "исключительно P.", the intensives each single-voice). Same overreach type as Kochergina's, but rarer: it is the opening simplification, not a recurring habit. **(2) Rare-before-common ordering.** Урок XLIV teaches the periphrastic future before the simple future against a 14:1 corpus frequency ratio (HB-58), and the -tavat active participle is billed "также часто" beside a passive twin 100× more frequent (HB-40). This is the paradigm-completeness tradition ordering by system rather than by text frequency — the same force behind Kochergina's frequency-blindness, a half-century earlier. **(3) The one stated frequency direction is flipped.** Bühler says the classical perfect is *rarer* than the imperfect (HB-57); DCS-2021 has the perfect 1.3× *more* frequent (61,986 vs ~47,554 tokens) — stratum-dependent (the aggregate is epic-heavy), so OVERSTATED rather than FALSE, and flagged per the D-B tie-break. The single FALSE (HB-60, the a-aorist "спрягается как impf. VII кл." beside a thematic paradigm) and the ā+Acc government slip (HB-10) both smell of print/translation errata rather than grammatical doctrine — the claims register and the errata register meet there.

Recommendation for the reading-site overlay: badge the six flagged entries and surface the thirteen M.G. frequency footnotes — Bühler states almost no numbers, and the corpus can now supply them (the ppp at 29.8% of all verbal tokens as the true center of the verb system, the optative as the second finite category at 9.3%, the -ya absolutive at 78.4% of the inventory). The cross-grammar layer is doing what D-A intended: with two registers live, presentation calibration — not factual accuracy — is where the grammars measurably differ.
