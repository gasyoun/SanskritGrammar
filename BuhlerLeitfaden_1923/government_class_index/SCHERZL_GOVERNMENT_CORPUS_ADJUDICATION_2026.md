### How the tiers are drawn (pre-registered, not fitted)

- **CONFIRMED** — a case-marked nominal is a direct dependency child of the verb.

- **CONTRADICTED** — the verb is well enough parsed to *see* its frame (≥20 parsed occurrences, ≥15 observed case-dependents), the Scherzl case appears in **zero** of those arcs, **and** it co-occurs with the verb at less than **half its chance rate** (cooc_ratio < 0.5; chance = verb_freq · corpus-base-rate of the case). Both gates are required so a sparse parse cannot manufacture a contradiction — a case that co-occurs heavily (e.g. jñā + genitive) is *never* called contradicted just because the parser missed the arc.

- **UNATTESTED-INSUFFICIENT** — no arc, and either the verb is too thinly parsed to judge or the case co-occurs at/above chance (weak positive) with no arc to confirm it.

- **NOT-ADJUDICABLE** — the root/stem string is not a DCS verb lemma.

## Limitations

- **NOT-ADJUDICABLE is not "Scherzl is wrong".** It is dominated by lemma-form mismatches — Scherzl lists the guṇa/attested stem (`sarj`, `darś`) while DCS keys the bare root (`sṛj`, `dṛś`). A stem→root normaliser (guṇa/vṛddhi/sandhi undo) plus the DCS `lemma` table's preverb column would recover most of these and is the clear follow-up.

- The **~4 % parse ceiling** means CONFIRMED counts are a floor and UNATTESTED-INSUFFICIENT is genuinely "unknown", not "absent". As DCS dependency coverage grows, rerun.

- CONTRADICTED is a **screening** verdict, hand-checked below; a homonym or preverb misjoin (the README names har/hṛ, idh/indh, kar/kṛ) can masquerade as one.
