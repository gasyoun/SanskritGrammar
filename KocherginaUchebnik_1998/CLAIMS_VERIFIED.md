## Methodology synthesis — are Kochergina's presentation principles justified?

On the full 40-lesson sweep, the headline of the verb-system pilot holds and sharpens: Kochergina's *factual accuracy is high* — the large majority of registered assertions are TRUE against the DCS-2021 corpus and Whitney 1889 — and her failures are almost never errors of fact but of *calibration*. Her presentation principle is the paradigm-completeness tradition of the Petersburg/Soviet school: give every form its rule and its exceptions, ordered by the grammatical system, and trust the learner to drill the tables. That principle is well-suited to accuracy and to reading the classical language, and it produces the many clean TRUE/JUSTIFIED anchors here (neuter N=A syncretism HK-13, one-final-consonant phonotactics HK-31, pronominal sarvam HK-30, the reduplication and passive-alignment rules).

Two systematic failure modes recur across the whole book. **(1) Universality overreach.** The beginner-facing rule is stated with an absolute quantifier — "всегда", "только", "по единому правилу" — where a well-known exception class exists. The sharpest case is HK-16 ("основы на -ī и -ū всегда женского рода"), which is simply FALSE: masculine ī/ū-stems (rathī, senānī, sudhī, monosyllabic bhū-) are well attested; she means the derived nadī/vadhū class. The same overreach drives HK-10 (accentless finite verb "никогда" — but it IS accented in a subordinate clause, Whitney §168), HK-14/HK-34 (a-/compound gender "always" the final/derived value — bahuvrīhi gender tracks the referent, contradicted in her own lesson), HK-25 (simple perfect "от всех односложных" — vid/ās/long-vowel roots take periphrastic), HK-38 (root aorist "от некоторых -ā и bhū" — the attested root-aorist set is much wider), and HK-40 (double-preverb ordering "всегда"). **(2) Frequency-blindness.** The tradition orders material by systematic regularity, not by how often a form is actually met, so a canonical "base rule" is foregrounded over the statistically dominant realisation of it. HK-4 (future -syá taught as the single rule while the seṭ -iṣya is 56.8% of forms) is the template; HK-15 (imperfect "обычно" the narrative past — stratum-dependent), HK-19 (class-IX "большинство носовой+согласная" — doubtful), HK-26, HK-32 all repeat it.

A third, smaller bucket is genuinely **UNTESTABLE**: the precise percentages Kochergina asserts without a stated count base — HK-41 (prefixed-verb 60/30/<10% semantic split) and HK-42 (a- privative ≥1/3 of prefixed nouns, ~80% abstract). Recommendation for the reading-site overlay: keep Kochergina's text intact (it is accurate) and badge the two failure modes — the FALSE/OVERSTATED universals with the counter-class, and the frequency-hidden claims with the corpus number — so the learner sees where an absolute rule has an escape hatch and where a "variant" is in fact the norm. The register generalises: the two axes are worth running on any grammar whose ordering predates corpus statistics (roadmap spine S1/S2).
